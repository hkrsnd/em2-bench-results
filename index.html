Framework

How SocialGrid Works

Inspired by Among Us, SocialGrid evaluates LLM agents in multi-agent, multi-objective environments across spatial planning, task execution, and adversarial social reasoning.

SocialGrid Overview. (Left) Users specify environment parameters (map size, room layout) and agent configurations. (Center) Agents operate under physical constraints; an optional Planning Oracle isolates social reasoning from navigation deficits. (Right) Multi-dimensional metrics across spatial, task, and social axes, with automated failure analysis and Elo-based rankings.
Results

Key Findings

Experiments across eight models (14B–120B) reveal fundamental limitations in spatial planning and social reasoning for embodied LLM agents.

🗺

Planning Bottleneck

Without assistance, most models struggle to even reach assigned tasks. GPT-OSS-120B, the strongest model, completes only ~50% of tasks unassisted. Planning efficiency rarely exceeds 0.5 even with the Planning Oracle.

<60%
Best model task completion without assist
🧠

Social Reasoning Fails

Even with the Planning Oracle lifting the navigation burden, agents fail to detect deception. Detection accuracy averages 29.9% across all league matchups—near or below the 33% random baseline.

29.9%
Avg detection accuracy (random = 33%)
🔍

Shallow Heuristics, Not Evidence

Analysis of 64,184 crewmate votes reveals agents rely on shallow cues ("erratic movement") rather than accumulating behavioral evidence. Over-trust affects 46.7% of votes, while weak heuristics dominate at 62.8%.

🎮

Impostors Dominate

Across league play, impostors win ~92.7% of matches, reflecting weak crewmate navigation: agents struggle to complete tasks before time runs out, giving impostors ample opportunity.

Analysis

Detailed Results

Task & Planning Performance

Assist vs No-Assist
Without assistance, most models struggle to even reach assigned tasks—GPT-OSS-120B completes only ~50% unassisted. With the Planning Oracle, all models improve, yet planning efficiency still falls short of 0.5 for most models.

Detection Accuracy Heatmap

Social Reasoning
Detection accuracy across 36 league matchups (30 cross-model + 6 self-play). All models perform near or below the random baseline (33%), averaging 29.9%.

Trust Calibration

Trust Metrics
Trust metrics for crewmate models facing GPT-OSS-120B as impostor. Brier Scores hover near the random baseline (0.33), and trust volatility indicates unstable, poorly-calibrated beliefs barely distinguishable from random guessing.

Complexity Analysis

Room Count vs Performance
Task and planning performance degrade linearly with room count (0.46 to 0.30), but voting accuracy hovers at ~33% regardless of complexity, confirming social reasoning failures are orthogonal to navigation difficulty.

Failure Analysis

Model-Specific Patterns
Radar charts showing six failure modes per model: passive failures (NOOP stall, task-fixation) dominate smaller models, while larger models exhibit active failures (door spam, backtracking). The Planning Oracle reduces failures by 90–97% for most models.

RL Training Progression

Reinforcement Learning
RL fine-tuning of Qwen3-4B via PPO yields minimal gains even after 2,500 updates in a simplified single-agent setup. This serves as a lower-bound feasibility check, suggesting spatial reasoning is a fundamental bottleneck that basic RL cannot easily overcome.
