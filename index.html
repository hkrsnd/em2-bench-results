Framework

How SocialGrid Works

Agents navigate gridworld environments, complete tasks, and participate in voting phases while building mental models of other agents.

SocialGrid Overview. Users specify environment parameters (map size, room layout) and agent configurations. Agents operate under physical constraints with an optional Planning Oracle to isolate social reasoning from navigation deficits. The system provides multi-dimensional metrics across spatial, task, and social axes.
Results

What We Discovered

Our evaluation reveals fundamental limitations in current LLM agents' embodied reasoning capabilities.

🗺

Planning Bottleneck

Without the planning assistant, models fail basic navigation - even struggling to reach assigned tasks. GPT-OSS-120B was the only model to achieve near-perfect task completion with assistance.

Analysis

Detailed Results

Task & Planning Performance

Assist vs No-Assist
Without the planning assistant, most models struggle to even reach assigned tasks. Planning efficiency rarely exceeds 0.5 even with assistance.

Voting Accuracy & Trust

Social Reasoning
Without assistance, voting accuracy never exceeds 60%. Trust Brier scores show agents frequently misjudge trustworthiness. Smaller models sometimes outperform larger ones.

Model Scaling Analysis

Parameters vs Performance
Strong positive correlation between parameters and composite score (r > 0.8), but detection accuracy shows minimal correlation with scale - all models perform near the 33% baseline.

Failure Analysis

Model-Specific Patterns
Passive failures (NOOP, task-fixation) dominate smaller models. Active failures (door spam, backtracking) appear in larger models. Planning assistant reduces failures by 90-97%.
