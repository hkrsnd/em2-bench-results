EM¬≤-Bench Overview

A controllable and embodied benchmark evaluating planning, reasoning, and psychological inference in multi-agent settings inspired by Among Us.

Figure 2: System Overview

Pipeline
Overview of EM¬≤-Bench. EM¬≤-Bench generates gridworld environments based on user-specified parameters, into which LLM agents are deployed and evaluated on both task completion and voting activities. Performance is automatically assessed and analyzed with actionable feedback for agent developers.
(Q1) Task & Planning Performance

Do major models complete their roles reliably in the embodied multi-agent multi-objective setting?

Current LLMs struggle with even basic path planning in embodied settings. Without the planning assistant, most models fail to reach their assigned tasks.

Figure 3: Task Performance & Planning Efficiency

Assist vs No-Assist
LLM agents cannot perform path planning reliably in the embodied setting. Comparison of crewmate performance on EM¬≤-Bench. Without the planning assistant, most models struggle‚Äîeven failing to reach assigned tasks. GPT-OSS-120B was the sole baseline to achieve near-perfect task completion with assistance. Planning efficiency rarely exceeds 0.5 even with assistance.
(Q2) Mental Model Building

Can LLM agents build mental models robustly, evaluating the trustworthiness of other agents reliably?

Trust Brier scores remain below 0.4 across all models, indicating frequent misjudgments about other agents' trustworthiness.

Figure 4: Voting Accuracy & Trust Assessment

Detection & Calibration
LLM agents cannot build mental models robustly in the embodied setting. Left: Voting accuracy‚Äîwithout assistance, performance never exceeds 60%. Right: Trust volatility‚Äîlarger models produce more stable assessments, but assistance doesn't consistently improve stability.

(Q3) Complexity Factors

What are the key factors that make models succeed or fail?

Map area and room count affect performance differently. Map area dominates task performance while room count affects planning efficiency more.

Figure 5: Complexity Analysis (No Assist)

TP & PP vs Environment Parameters
Complexity analysis (No Assist): task performance (TP) and planning performance (PP). Without assistance, all models struggle significantly. Performance degrades with increased map complexity. Map area shows stronger negative correlation with TP (Œ≤=-0.12), while room count more strongly affects PP (Œ≤=-0.22).
(Q4) Error Analysis

What typical failures do the agents make in the environment?

LLM agents make systematic failures including door spam, stalling, and backtracking loops‚Äîbehaviors rarely seen in human players.

Systematic Failure Modes
LLM agents make systematic failures in the embodied setting. Top-k models shown with the covered fraction of failures out of the entire episode.
